Mission Journal

Germany Hamburg, April 10, 1996 - April 4, 1998
Home Journal

I'm transcribing my mission journal on the day it happened 30 years later.

I wrote in my journal every day on my mission. Well, to be more accurate, if I missed a day, I wrote about it the next day. Every day was accounted for.

Why? I wanted to write a very basic Python static site generator for a while, and since I started my mission 30 years ago, I thought this was a perfect excuse to do that. So I made pysite.

I left out full names and sometimes use a different letter to represent someone's real name. Just in case. I also fixed most grammar issues. My spelling was atrocious. I left most of the mistakes in German grammar and spelling in place, because it's cute, like a little kid learning to talk. And I thought it would be cool to see the progression as I got better. Sometimes I'll add some present day commentary to the bottom of the post.

Orange Eggs and Ham & my first Döner

June 18, 1996

Today went alright. I made ham and eggs for breakfast. I've never seen orange eggs until today. I kept on falling asleep during personal study time, so I didn't get much done. Today was Elders C, H and me for the whole day. Elder H's comp was at Golden Conference all day. In fact, he still hasn't returned as of 21:43. We did a bit of street contacting until 11:00 or so, and then came back so Elder C could finish the Institute lesson. I cleaned the kitchen, all the dishes and the sink. Then tried to study, and I woke up just before Elder C asked if would like to fix dinner, or lunch rather. I made a bunch of spaghetti and some spaghetti sauce. I thought it was alright, but I had too much and almost puked by the time I was done. Then I tried to study some more after finishing the lunch dishes, and woke up a few minutes before we left again. This time we tried to look up a few old contacts, but nobody was home, so we did street contacts with no luck until Institute time. I normally think of Institute as for college age persons, but since there are only a few in the ward that age, anyone can come. So at Institute today was B, Brüder J, Schwester B and one other Schwester. I can't remember her name. That went pretty well. Tons of candy! We stayed after to help B with her applications to be a Nanny. We were there for quite a while. On the way home we got Döners. It's like a fajita with lamb meat and sheep cheese. It was awesome tasting. My feet hurt and stink so it must have been a good day.

Oh, man. I can not say enough goodness about Döners. I would fly to Germany just to get a Döner. They are my absolute favorite food.
