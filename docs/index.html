Germany Hamburg, April 10, 1996 - April 4, 1998
Home Journal

I'm transcribing my mission journal on the day it happened 30 years later.

I wrote in my journal every day on my mission. Well, to be more accurate, if I missed a day, I wrote about it the next day. Every day was accounted for.

Why? I wanted to write a very basic Python static site generator for a while, and since I started my mission 30 years ago, I thought this was a perfect excuse to do that. So I made pysite.

I left out full names and sometimes use a different letter to represent someone's real name. Just in case. I also fixed most grammar issues. My spelling was atrocious. I left most of the mistakes in German grammar and spelling in place, because it's cute, like a little kid learning to talk. And I thought it would be cool to see the progression as I got better. Sometimes I'll add some present day commentary to the bottom of the post.

Hail the size of marbles

May 30, 1996

Heute war ein gute Tag. Der Wetter war sehr komisch. Ein Minuten es war Sonne scheinn, und dann, die nexte minute es war Hageling. What I mean to say, is that the weather was weird today. First was a nice sun shinney day. Then all of a sudden clouds poured in, an it started hailing, big ones, the size of marbles. That only lasted for about an five minutes. That was during the mid-day class. During the evening class, the weather got all cloudy again and it down poured for a few minutes, then turned all sunny again. But just for a few minutes, because then it was nighttime, and darkness fell. The day went fine for me. I didn't speak as much German as I would have like to. I really need to work on it. It's just frustrating, because I don't know how to say everything that I want to, and nobody else really seems to try too hard. Oh well. Bis Später.
