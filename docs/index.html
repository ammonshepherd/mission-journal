Mission Journal

Germany Hamburg, April 10, 1996 - April 4, 1998
Home Journal

I'm transcribing my mission journal on the day it happened 30 years later.

I wrote in my journal every day on my mission. Well, to be more accurate, if I missed a day, I wrote about it the next day. Every day was accounted for.

Why? I wanted to write a very basic Python static site generator for a while, and since I started my mission 30 years ago, I thought this was a perfect excuse to do that. So I made pysite.

I left out full names and sometimes use a different letter to represent someone's real name. Just in case. I also fixed most grammar issues. My spelling was atrocious. I left most of the mistakes in German grammar and spelling in place, because it's cute, like a little kid learning to talk. And I thought it would be cool to see the progression as I got better. Sometimes I'll add some present day commentary to the bottom of the post.

Tangent time

June 01, 1996

Today was an awesome day. I stayed with Elder G in the morning, and I slept the whole time. Brother J's class was kind of dumb. I like him and all, it's just that Elder J and K are always getting Brother J of on tangents. The tangents are things that are good to know, but we didn't do much of what we were supposed to. The first hour of class was tangent time and a bit of the second half of class. All in all, it's alright.

I finally saw J.M. at dinner. We talked for a while.

The whole day was just cool. Elder R and I had a lot of fun. He taught 1.4 and 1.5 of the discussions to me auf Deutsch. It was awesome, I even felt the Spirit. We've decided to wear one tie until we leave. I bought an awesome tie that has a bunch of flags on it. He and I are the only ones that like it. So he's going to buy one and we're both going to wear our ties for the rest of the time we're here. It'll be cool. Well, that's all for now. Bis Später.

Some guys from back home.
