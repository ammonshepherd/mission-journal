Mission Journal

Germany Hamburg, April 10, 1996 - April 4, 1998
Home Journal

I'm transcribing my mission journal on the day it happened 30 years later.

I wrote in my journal every day on my mission. Well, to be more accurate, if I missed a day, I wrote about it the next day. Every day was accounted for.

Why? I wanted to write a very basic Python static site generator for a while, and since I started my mission 30 years ago, I thought this was a perfect excuse to do that. So I made pysite.

I left out full names and sometimes use a different letter to represent someone's real name. Just in case. I also fixed most grammar issues. My spelling was atrocious. I left most of the mistakes in German grammar and spelling in place, because it's cute, like a little kid learning to talk. And I thought it would be cool to see the progression as I got better. Sometimes I'll add some present day commentary to the bottom of the post.

A sky full of clouds

May 25, 1996

Today was alright. The weather, I think, was the awesomest part of the day. For most of the morning, until about noon, the sky was just complete clouds. It was really neat looking. For most of the day too the mountain outside the window, the one behind the temple, was just covered in clouds. It was really awesome. There was tons of dark patches and light patches all over. Also cool about today was that we did a ton of door approaches. It was all in German, too. We did it with our sister district. It was a little difficult to try and think up a way that was good and not just "Hi, I'm Elder Shepherd and I'm a missionary... And I have a message about Christ for you." It helped, though, to make me think of other ways to bring up the topic of Christ. I can't wait to get to Germany. Only an Arizona boy could find a sky full of clouds all day as something "really neat looking".
