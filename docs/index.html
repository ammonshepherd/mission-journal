Mission Journal

Germany Hamburg, April 10, 1996 - April 4, 1998
Home Journal

I'm transcribing my mission journal on the day it happened 30 years later.

I wrote in my journal every day on my mission. Well, to be more accurate, if I missed a day, I wrote about it the next day. Every day was accounted for.

Why? I wanted to write a very basic Python static site generator for a while, and since I started my mission 30 years ago, I thought this was a perfect excuse to do that. So I made pysite.

I left out full names and sometimes use a different letter to represent someone's real name. Just in case. I also fixed most grammar issues. My spelling was atrocious. I left most of the mistakes in German grammar and spelling in place, because it's cute, like a little kid learning to talk. And I thought it would be cool to see the progression as I got better. Sometimes I'll add some present day commentary to the bottom of the post.

Westwood MTC journals

June 08, 1996

Only three more days left. Today was really awesome. We, Elder R, G, taught the first discussion to a former Sister that Bro. S brought in for us. It was a tad hard. Elder G did most of the talking. I bore my testimony and almost started crying. Sister B, D's old teacher, taught us this afternoon. It was just Elders' R, G, R, K and I. It was a pretty cool class.

I had a couple of prayers answered today. I was praying hard in my heart to find Elder W or someone who knew where Westwood Journals were. I finally saw J.W. and he said Elder K, had them. At lunch I wasn't even expecting to see him, but out of the corner of my eye I saw Elder K. I chased him down, but he didn't have the books with him. I'm hoping and praying now, that I'll be able to get them before I fly. The best thing about all this was that I've got another example for me how Heavenly Father answers prayers. It really strengthened my testimony on prayer.
