Germany Hamburg, April 10, 1996 - April 4, 1998
Home Journal

I'm transcribing my mission journal on the day it happened 30 years later.

I wrote in my journal every day on my mission. Well, to be more accurate, if I missed a day, I wrote about it the next day. Every day was accounted for.

Why? I wanted to write a very basic Python static site generator for a while, and since I started my mission 30 years ago, I thought this was a perfect excuse to do that. So I made pysite.

I left out full names and sometimes use a different letter to represent someone's real name. Just in case. I also fixed most grammar issues. My spelling was atrocious. I left most of the mistakes in German grammar and spelling in place, because it's cute, like a little kid learning to talk. And I thought it would be cool to see the progression as I got better. Sometimes I'll add some present day commentary to the bottom of the post.

Today was a good success

June 07, 1996

Today was a totally awesome P-day. I had an awesome session at the temple and I also did almost 100 baptisms for the dead. It was cool. While I was waiting to be baptized I read an article in the Ensign about the Holy Ghost. It helped me out a ton. I know I felt it a lot today. We had an awesome testimony meeting tonight. I was able to feel the Spirit and feel even closer to my district. I'm so excited to leave this place and get to Germany. Being here in the MTC doesn't feel like a mission, just like going off to school. I can't wait to get going on the "real" mission. I feel today was a good success. I was able to feel the Spirit, and have a cool day. The only thing I wish I would do more, for today and forever, is read more in the Book of Mormon. It seems that I can never find time to read. I hope there will be more time, at least to read, while in the field.
